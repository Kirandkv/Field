# Data Card — FieldForge Industries Synthetic Corpus

## Summary

All data in `data/samples/` and generated by `data/generators/` describes a **fictional**
industrial operator, "FieldForge Industries," and its fleet of mobile inspection robots and
fixed environmental sensors. No real company, employer, customer, incident, or individual is
represented. This card covers two corpora: the document corpus (manuals, SOPs, inspection report,
maintenance log — FieldForge Docs) and the telemetry corpus (device registry, sensor time series,
alert scenarios — FieldForge Copilot). The two are deliberately consistent: the flagship telemetry
incident (`data/samples/telemetry/alerts.json`, `alert-2026-06-14`) matches the narrative already
written into `data/samples/inspection_report_2026_06_14_site_b.md`.

## Data-generation process

- Authored/templated directly by the repo maintainer (with AI assistance) as plain-text/Markdown
  documents, matching realistic structure for industrial technical manuals, safety SOPs, and
  inspection reports.
- No scraping, no real manuals, no employer material of any kind was used as a source.
- Every document is versioned in `data/samples/` under git, so provenance is the commit history
  itself.
- Telemetry (`data/samples/telemetry/*.jsonl`) is produced by
  `data/generators/generate_telemetry.py` with a fixed random seed: a Gaussian baseline
  (mean 10 ppm, std 2 ppm) per device over a 105-day window, with four hand-placed
  incident windows (spikes and one deliberate data gap) layered on top. Deterministic —
  re-running the generator reproduces byte-identical output, so eval gold outcomes never drift.

## Schemas

Ingested documents map to the `Document` / `DocumentPage` / `Chunk` contracts in
`packages/contracts/fieldforge_contracts`. Telemetry maps to `Device` / `TelemetryPoint` / `Alert`
in `packages/contracts/fieldforge_contracts/copilot_models.py`. See those modules for exact fields.

## Intended uses

- Exercising and evaluating the FieldForge Docs retrieval/answer pipeline and the FieldForge
  Copilot investigation/approval pipeline.
- Producing the eval datasets in `evals/datasets/`.
- Portfolio demonstration only.

## Out-of-scope uses

- Not intended as a source of real industrial-safety guidance. Do not use SOP content in this
  repo for actual field operations.
- Not a benchmark dataset for third-party model evaluation beyond this project's own scope.

## Limitations

- Small corpus (single digits of documents in slice 1) — retrieval metrics are indicative for
  this project's own regression testing, not statistically representative of production-scale
  document collections.
- English-only in slice 1. A bilingual English/Arabic manual is planned for M2 alongside the
  cross-lingual retrieval eval subset described in the program brief; it does not exist yet.
- Synthetic text style may be more uniform/regular than real technical writing (fewer OCR
  artifacts, fewer inconsistent formats) — this is a known synthetic-data artifact. OCR-noise
  and scan-artifact simulation are planned (M2), not present in slice 1's native-text corpus.
- Telemetry covers exactly 3 devices and 1 metric (methane ppm). Isolation Forest is trained on a
  single-feature series per device — real deployments would use multivariate features (rate of
  change, time-of-day, battery, etc.); this is a disclosed simplification, not a hidden one.
- The four telemetry incident scenarios were placed by hand to exercise specific classification
  branches (real event, sensor fault ×2, insufficient evidence), not sampled from a real incident
  distribution — they are test fixtures, not a claim about real-world incident base rates.

## Biases

- Equipment, incident, and procedure types reflect the maintainer's own domain background
  (robotics, sensors, gas/environmental monitoring) rather than a survey of the industrial sector
  broadly. Treated as a deliberate, disclosed choice, not an oversight.

## Languages

- Slice 1: English only.
- Planned: Arabic (bilingual manual + cross-lingual eval subset), matching the suite's stated
  multilingual scope.

## Privacy considerations

- Zero real personal data. Any names, employee IDs, or contact details appearing in sample
  maintenance tickets/logs are fictional placeholders, not drawn from any real person or system.
- The guardrail PII/secret regex layer (see [threat model](docs/threat-model/THREAT_MODEL.md))
  is tested against this corpus specifically to confirm it does *not* false-negative on the
  intentionally-planted adversarial fixtures in the guardrail eval set.

## Known synthetic-data artifacts

- Section numbering and formatting are more consistent than typical real-world manuals.
- No scanned-page artifacts (skew, noise, watermark bleed) in slice 1 — all documents are
  native digital text, not scans, until the OCR-degradation simulator (M2) is built.
